About

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Computer Science at Texas Tech University, where I hold Teaching Assistant and Distinguished Graduate Student Assistantships. My research focuses on Quantum Machine Learning, with particular interest in quantum neural networks, quantum physics-informed neural networks (QPINNs), quantum kernel methods, and quantum optimization, as well as applications in financial technology.

Before returning to research, I spent more than a decade in industry and education — twelve years as a software engineer, business analyst and project manager at FPT Software (with onsite delivery in Malaysia and the United States), followed by six years as an instructor and senior teacher at FPT University. This combination of engineering practice and teaching shapes how I approach research problems today.
